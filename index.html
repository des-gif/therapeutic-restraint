The approach

Restraint is the last resort — never the starting point.

We help staff understand behaviour before they respond, so physical intervention is used only when truly necessary — and always calmly, proportionately and with the person's dignity at the centre.

Our model

A therapeutic model, not traditional restraint training.

Our training is built around prevention, Positive Behaviour Support, de-escalation, trauma-informed care, adverse childhood experiences and adult mental health awareness. Staff learn to understand behaviour as communication — so when physical intervention is unavoidable, it's delivered safely, lawfully and with compassion.
